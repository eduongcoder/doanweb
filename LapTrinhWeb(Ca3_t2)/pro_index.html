Naruto[a] is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Kishimoto Masashi. The manga was first serialized in Weekly Shonen Jump from September 1999 to November 2014, and was later released by Shueisha into 72 tankōbon volumes. The story revolves around Uzumaki Naruto, A young ninja wants to find a way to assert himself to be recognized by everyone and pursue his dream of becoming Hokage - leader of Konoha Village. The plot is divided into two parts – the first part is set a few years before adolescence (Naruto Dattebayo) and the second part is in Naruto's teenage years (Naruto Shippuden). The series is based on two of Kishimoto's Yomikiri: Karakuri (1995), which helped Kishimoto win Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto (1997). Naruto was serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump, Shueisha's magazine from 1999 to 2014, and was packaged in tankōbon with 72 volumes.
